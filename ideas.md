Conway's Game of Life only has:

Dead cell
Alive cell

So each cell has 2 states.

You can generalize it to:

Red species
Blue species
Green species
Predator
Prey
Food
Dead

Or even 100 different states.

For example:

Competitive Life

Two species compete for territory.

Rules might be:

Empty cell becomes red if surrounded by mostly red.
Empty cell becomes blue if surrounded by mostly blue.
Cells die if overcrowded.

This creates moving borders and wars.

Predator-Prey Life

Think wolves and rabbits.

Rules:

Rabbits reproduce rapidly.
Wolves eat rabbits.
Wolves starve without food.

You get oscillating populations similar to real ecosystems.

Cyclic Dominance

Rock-paper-scissors as a cellular automaton.

Red kills Blue
Blue kills Green
Green kills Red

This creates beautiful spiral patterns.

Chemical Life

You can model reactions.

Instead of "alive" and "dead":

A chemical
B chemical
C chemical

Each reacts with neighbors.

This leads to patterns resembling:

Coral
Zebra stripes
Bacterial colonies
Chemical waves

Some real organisms are thought to use similar mechanisms during development.

The really cool part

Once you have enough states, you're no longer making a "Game of Life."

You're making a universe.

Each cell becomes:

A location
With matter
Following local physics

And then you just watch what emerges.

People have found:

Gliders
Oscillators
Self-replicators
Logic gates
Ecosystems
Evolution

all from stupidly simple local rules.
